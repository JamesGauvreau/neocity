Ignition Hex

Incantation: Lacarnum Inflamari. Light: Orange. Taught in: 5th year DADA. 

Effect: Sets the (non-living) target object on fire, especially clothing.

Other information: Classified as a hex because it is designed to get around Fire-Proofing Charms. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration.

Illegibility Charm

Incantation: Illegibilus. 

Effect: Makes a written passage impossible to read.

Other information: Often used to encode messages which can only be “re-legiblized” with a particular counter-incantation. Sufficiently powerful counter-charms can still break the enchantment but a skilled wix can leave a particular distinctive pattern (perhaps the numerical values of the fourth, twelfth, and twenty-ninth letters in any encoded message will add up to a specific sum) that is not in itself magical, so a snooping intermediary will need to know both (1) the checksum and (2) the original caster’s variation on the Illegibility Charm in order to properly re-encode the message in a manner which will hide the fact their intrusion. The Voynich Manuscript is the product of a (yet-unbroken) Illegibility Charm of great power.

Source(s): Canon, with significant elaboration.

Imperius Curse

Incantation: Imperio. 

Effect: Places the target under the control of the caster, by suppressing their will beneath the caster's.

Other information: There are anti-Imperius countermeasures, but they are generally stationary (e.g. Thief's Downfall) or take some time to kick in (e.g. prolonged Dementor exposure). The strongest defense against the Imperius is that it's just plain hard to perform, especially at scale. Imperius'd wixes can Imperius other wixes, but it's very difficult to pull that off: not only are you suppressing the wills of two people at once, but you're trying to suppress the second with the first, and if someone doesn't have the strength to resist you then they're unlikely to have the strength to dominate someone else. Some wixes take a different approach. Rather than overpower the victim's will, they try to charm the victim in a muggle sense, using the Imperius Curse to create a mental connection and make the victim a little more impressionable, and then presenting the "command" as though it's the victim's own idea. This has its own limitations, of course, and it, too, becomes more difficult as you add links to the chain (though less quickly). Other mind-altering charms cannot be used through, and are broken by, the Imperius Curse. 

Source(s): Canon, with significant elaboration, including from Witchcraft & Wizardry and Blood Crest, by Cauchy.

Imperturbable Charm

Incantation: Impervius. Taught in: 4th year Charms. 

Effect: Creates an invisible barrier around an object, such as a door or pair of glasses, which repels minor disruptions like lightly-thrown objects, noises, and water.

Variability: Can be specialized for specific elements or materials. 

Other information: It is no help against a serious attempt at intrusion, even by non-magical methods. When cast on a person, typically affects clothing but not held objects. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from myself and from Hogwarts is Here. There’s an “Imperturbable Charm” and an “Impervius” incantation, whose effects have a certain similarity to each other.

Index Conjuration

Incantation: Unknown.

Effect: With reference to a set of documents, conjures a list of those documents.

Variability: Can be adapted to index specific items, e.g. every occurrence of the word "indigo," or a series of specific items, e.g. every occurrence of a color word, with page numbers grouped into the seven Newtonian Colors and every other color word redirecting to the closest appropriate Newtonian Color.

Source(s): Proposed by Taure in a DLP thread, with some elaboration.

Instant Scalping Hex

Incantation: Implumus. 

Effect: Removes all hair from the target.

Variability: Typically affects head hair, but can be modified to affect facial hair or all hair, or to affect very specific parts of the head.

Source(s): Canon, with an incantation from Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Intruder Charm

Incantation: Entrusis.

Effect: The caster's wand emits a quiet alarm when the target threshold is crossed. 

Variability: Characteristics of the alarm (e.g. duration, tone, volume) may be modified. The default alarm is a soft hum seven seconds in length, which even the caster may fail to notice if they are distracted.

Source(s): Hogwarts is Here, with some elaboration. 

Ironing Charm

Incantation: Levigo. 

Effect: Heats a flatiron and enchants it to iron a pile of laundry, remove unwanted wrinkles, and put creases in the right places. 

Variability: The caster's wand may be used in place of a flatiron. The spell may be used to enchant Self-Ironing Robes. 

Source(s): Witchcraft & Wizardry.
