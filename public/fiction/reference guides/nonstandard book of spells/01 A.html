Age Line Charm

Incantation: Anno Invius. 

Effect: Prevents individuals below a specific age from crossing a specific point or entering a specific area. Might be able to prevent individuals above a specific age from doing the same.

Other information: Relies on calendar age, not physical age. Might be a jinx, if it also inflicts an effect on someone who tries to cross it, but the spontaneous beards exhibited by Fred and George Weasley (and two others) might have been the result of a second spell and/or been produced by an interaction with the Ageing Potion that they used. 

Source(s): Canon, with incantation from Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Anti-Apparition Charm

Incantation: Potest Intoir. 

Effect: Put generally, an Anti-Apparition Charm prevents anyone from safely Apparating into the warded space.

Variability: Possible effects include displacing the traveler to another location, forcefully bouncing them back, or splinching them. Some Anti-Apparition Charms intentionally provide a "warning signal" of some kind, and others are intentionally "quiet" but may nevertheless be noticed ahead of time by a sufficiently-aware would-be traveler. It is simpler to cast upon an enclosed space, or at least a space that has been "delimited" or marked out (e.g. chalk circle).

Other information: Cast on Hogwarts and the grounds thereof to prevent Apparition, but regularly lifted in order to permit Apparition lessons by Sixth-Year students. Distinct from the Anti-Disapparition Jinx. Does not apply to House-Elf Apparition. Might not apply to Goblin Apparition. 

Source: Canon, with some elaboration from Hogwarts is Here and myself.

Anti-Choking Charm (Vanishing)

A.K.A. Airway-clearing Charm. Learned in: Unknown, but used by a 5th year during the 1988-89 school year.

Incantation: Anapneo, meaning "I breathe."

Effect: A kind of Vanishing Spell which only works properly in a living body, because that body helps to set the limits of the spell. When cast properly, the Anti-Choking Spell removes all foreign matter from a particular region of the body—typically the throat, but the skilled practitioner can use it to clear other parts of the body as well. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration. 

Anti-Disapparition Jinx

Incantation: Unknown. 

Effect: Prevents or otherwise disrupts Disapparition. 

Variability: Some modifications may dislocate would-be Disapparitionists to a particular location (e.g. beside the caster) rather than block Disapparition entirely. Most competent wixes can cover 1,000 square feet (roughly a 2-3 bedroom house in the U.K.) with a single application of the spell, especially if it is an enclosed space. It will usually last for a few hours, but with greater skill and technique can preserve it for much longer, while a sloppy casting might wear off in ten or fifteen minutes. 

Other information: Cast on Hogwarts and the grounds thereof to prevent Disapparition, but regularly lifted in order to permit Apparition lessons by Sixth-Year students. Does not apply to House-Elf Disapparition. Might not apply to Goblin Disapparition. 

Source(s): Canon, with significant elaboration. 

Anti-Intruder Jinx

Incantation: Unknown.

Effect: Mechanism and effects unknown, though at least one kind of Anti-Intruder Jinx would, it was implied, prevent Harry Potter from climbing the Entrance Gates at Hogwarts after it were applied in Harry's Sixth Year.

Other information: Might refer to a whole category of spells. 

Source(s): Canon.

Anti-Unlocking Charm

Incantation: Obstructus Impervium.

Effect: Protects the target lock against spells which might unlock it.

Variability: Alone, the charm is only moderately useful, but for every form of unlocking spell there is (at least in theory) a variation of the Anti-Unlocking Charm that is tailored specifically to that spell. With sufficient knowledge and patience, as well as proper technique, it is possible to make a lock impregnable against every unlocking spell the caster can imagine—but no more than that. 

Source(s): Witchcraft & Wizardry, with some elaboration. 

Antler-Growing Jinx

Incantation: Anteoculatia, from Spanish ante, meaning "buckskin" and Latin oculas, meaning "eyes." Light: Red or purple.

Effect: Causes large horns to grow from the target's head.

Other information: Possibly takes a long time to treat, because Pansy Parkinson missed a day's worth of classes after she was hit by this spell.  

Source(s): Canon.

Apparition

Incantation: None. Signs: Creates an audible noise, anywhere from a small pop to a loud crack; personal physical discomfort.  

Effect: Causes the user to travel instantly from one location to another. Requires that the user mentally focus on the desired location (i.e. Destination, Determination, and Deliberation). 

Variability: Becomes more difficult with distance, and inter-continental Apparition is discouraged. It is possible to bring someone with you when Apparating, which is called Side-Along Apparition.  

Other information: It is probable that one must have been to a location before in order to Apparate there effectively.  Most people vomit the first time they Apparate, and the general discomfort associated with it may be why many wixes prefer other methods of travel. 

Source(s): Canon.

Ascension Charm

A.K.A. Ascending Charm, Propelling Charm, Propulsion Charm

Incantation: Ascendio. 

Effect: Lifts the caster high into the air; or, in water, propels the caster to the surface. 

Miscasting: Properly cast, this spell will allow the caster to return to the ground unharmed, but novices are likely to injure themselves if not monitored. 

Other information: This charm cannot be used to influence the caster's flight path after taking off. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration and alternate names from various sources, including Witchcraft & Wizardry.
