Mechanical Locking Charm

Incantation: Declaustrum.

Effect: Activates an existing mechanical lock, regardless of its complexity. 

Other information: The lock is sealed non-magically, meaning that keys and lockpicks will be sufficient to open it.

Memory Extraction Spell

A.K.A. Memory-extracting Charm. 

Incantation: Animus Exorire.

Effect: Withdraws a memory in physical form.

Variability: It is possible to withdraw another person's memories by means of this spell, but much more difficult. Using it on an unwilling target is also possible, but sufficiently difficult that it is nearly always more practical to use Legilimency and then withdraw one's own memories. 

Other information: Memories are withdrawn in the form of "Mneme," a light, fragile, and highly soluble silver-colored substance that will adhere to the wand until the caster's concentration breaks. Under most circumstances, Mneme dissipates into the air unless quickly jarred or otherwise contained. If cast wandlessly, Mneme will typically exit the body through the eyes or another orifice. The use of this spell will moderately impair the target's personal recollection, but viewing of the extracted memory in a Pensieve will address this. Mneme is more easily affected by the False Memory Charm than memories that are still held inside a living mind. 

Source(s): Something like this must clearly exist in canon, but the details have been filled out by myself, Hogwarts is Here, and Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Mending Charm

A.K.A. Repairing Charm, Object-mending Charm

Incantation: Reparo. 

Effect: Fixes a broken object.

Variability: Other charms are required to undo the effects of natural weathering or restore the magical functions of a broken object. When applied to the same object on multiple occasions, the Mending Charm becomes less effective. If used on living creatures, it can result in scarring or other disfigurement, so it is illegal to do so.

Other information: May be specified as the Object-Mending Charm, to avoid confusion with the Knit-Up Charm (which is sometimes referred to as the Mending Charm). Requires visualization of the repair. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from The Arithmancer, by White Squirrel (ch. link), and from Hogwarts is Here and Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Muffling Charm

Incantation: Muffliato. 

Effect: Produces a mild buzzing sound in the ears of nearby listeners, which covers up conversations being held between the caster and anyone who has been excluded from the charm's effect.

Variability: The natural tendency of a voice is to be heard, and so this tendency must be opposed by the spell and the caster's will. Those that are figuratively soft-spoken will find the spell easier than those that are boisterous, regardless of their actual volume. 

Source(s): Canon, with original incantation and some elaboration from myself and Hogwarts is Here.

Muggle-repelling Charm

Incantation: Repello Muggletum.

Effect: Muggles are unable to see the enchanted area or object for what it is — typically perceiving ruins and decay instead — and if they attempt to enter or interact with it, they suddenly remember some pressing engagement elsewhere. 

Variability: It is possible to alter how Muggles perceive the enchanted area or object, and what they feel upon trying to enter or interact with it. 

Source(s): Canon. 

Multi-Shot Jinx

Incantation: Palmatefy, a portmanteau of palmate (an extended reference to the duck’s foot gun) and stupefy. Light: Red.

Effect: Fires multiple Stunning bolts, after the manner of the Stunning Charm.

Other information: Rather tricky to cast—often simpler to just cast multiple Stunning Charms. Classified as a “jinx” because there’s no practical use for it outside of duels.

Source(s): Canon, with original incantation and some elaboration.
