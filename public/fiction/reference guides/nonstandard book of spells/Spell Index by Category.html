Antispells and counterspells

Counterspells affect spells that have already been cast, while antispells prevent the spell from being used at all. Some spells are both, though they are usually more effective at one task or the other. 

Different spells have different counters, and broader counterspells will always be less effective against a particular spell than a more specialized counterspell would be.

Inverse Charms

When two charms can perfectly counteract and undo each other's effects, they are called inverse charms. If just one counteracts the other, which cannot do the same to the first, then that first spell is called a one-way inverse charm.
