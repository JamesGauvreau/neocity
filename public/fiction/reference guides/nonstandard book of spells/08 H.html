Hair-loss Curse

Incantation: Calvorio. Light: Red or purple.

Effect: Causes the target's hair to fall out.

Source(s): Canon.

Hairstyling Spell

Incantation: Crinus Muto. Light: White.

Effect: Changes the color and style of the caster's hair, including facial hair.

Other information: Hair length and body hair are not alterable by this spell. 

Source(s): Canon, with elaboration from Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Hair-thickening Charm

Incantation: Cappilus. 

Effect: Causes the target’s hair to grow longer and thicker in a short period of time.

Variability: Can target particular locations (e.g. head hair, eyebrows), and may cause excessive or improperly-placed growth if cast poorly.

Source(s): Canon, with an incantation from Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Hardening Charm

Incantation: Duro. Taught in: 3rd year Charms.

Effect: Hardens the target object, making it stiff and dense like stone, but no brittler than before.

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from Hogwarts is Here.

Head-Shrinking Curse

Incantation: Redactum Skullus. Light: Red or purple.

Effect: Shrinks the skull and surrounding tissue of the target's head.

Other information: Can be used to counter some uses of the Engorgement Charm and similar spells.

Source(s): Canon.

Hiccough Jinx

Incantation: Lóxigkas. 

Effect: Makes a person hiccup.

Other information: Curiously, if cast on a person who is already hiccupping for non-magical reasons, their hiccups will be cured. In Britain, this would be called a Hiccough Jinx. 

Source(s): Alexandra Quick and the Thorn Circle (ch. link), by Inverarity, with some elaboration. 

Hiccough-curing Charm

Incantation: Perultus. 

Effect: Cures hiccoughs / hiccups.

Other information: Should never be used on a case of hiccups that has persisted for over 48 hours, or to cure a recurring case of hiccups. If used on someone without hiccups, it may produce an uncomfortable feeling in the stomach.   

Source(s): Witchcraft & Wizardry and Hogwarts is Here.

Hidden Homes Charm

Incantation: Cave Inimicum.

Effect: Conceals people and objects within the targeted space from the perception of those who are neither the caster nor within that space at the time of the casting. 

Other information: Though the spell bears a strong similarity to the Fidelius Charm(particularly in the way that one might be concealed from another despite standing beside them), it is capable of being broken or otherwise thwarted in any number of ways. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from Hogwarts is Here.

Homorphus Charm

Incantation: Homorpho. 

Effect: Returns a transfigured person to their original form.

Variability: May temporarily make further transfiguration of the target more difficult or even impossible.

Other information: Despite rumors to the contrary, even a master of the spell cannot hope to affect a werewolf under the lunar influence.

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from myself and Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Horrifying Hex

Incantation: Threnoidia. 

Effect: Causes the target to hallucinate according to their deepest fears, not unlike a boggart.

Other information: It is important to note that this spell does not inflict any kind of compulsion to believe the hallucination.

Source(s): Original.

Other information: The spell does not move the target except vertically, but the target may move under its own power under some circumstances, and may be moved around by other forces (such as a breeze, if the target is small).

Human-Presence Revealing Charm

A.K.A. Human-Revealing Spell.

Incantation: Homenum Revelio. Light: White sparks.

Effect: Alerts the caster to the presence of humans in the indicated area. For each human thus detected, a spark will leap from the tip of the wand to one's hand. 

Variability: May target one's surroundings, a specific location, etc., generally to a limit of fifty square feet. It is possible to cast an unlit version of this spell, in which merely the (tingling, unpainful) sensation of sparks is felt. 

Other information: Generally felt by revealed targets as a "swooping" sensation, but it is possible to cast this spell in an undetectable or less detectable manner. A form of the Revealing Charm, but sufficiently complex to be taught separately.

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from myself and Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Hurling Hex

Incantation: Everte Statum, from everte, "to throw out," and statum, "stand," suggesting that the target is thrown from a standing position. 

Effect: Causes the target object (whether a broom or a chair) to attempt to hurl its user.

Other information: Can be imbued in an object, unlike Quirrell’s jinx.

Source(s): Canon, though it's worth noting that this entry conflates the Hurling Hex and the incantation Everte Statum.
