Wand-extinguishing Charm

Incantation: Nox. Taught in: 1st Year Charms.

Effect: Extinguishes the illumination produced by the Wand-Lighting Charm, as well as (with greater skill) other sources of "wandlight."

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration.

Wand-lighting Charm

Incantation: Lumos. Light: Variable, but white by default. Taught in: 1st Year Charms, DADA.

Effect: Illuminates the tip of the caster's wand.

Variability: Some casters can illuminate their wand without actually holding it, and/or fling the light away from their wands and subsequently sustain the light for some time.

Other information: The light produced is warm and can potentially set a fire (including the caster's own wand). It is white by default but can be altered. Novice wixes will often have amend the incantation, e.g. Lumos Rosea in order to produce a pink wandlight. Other common pendants include Rubeo (red), Luteo (orange), Flavus (yellow), Viridus (green), Caerulo (blue), and Ostrinus (purple).

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Wand-silencing Charm

Incantation: Pax. 

Effect: Negates spells like the Whistling Wand Charm.

Variability: Proficient casters can silence other wands with this spell. 

Source(s): Hogwarts is Here.

Water-making Spell

Incantation: Aguamenti. Light: Icy blue. Taught in: 6th Year Charms.

Effect: Conjures a jet of clean, drinkable water from the tip of the caster's wand.

Variability: The water pressure is generally light, but can be high enough to knock someone down or even cut another material, as with a waterjet. Changing the temperature is also possible, and some wixes can even use this spell to produce ice, now, or steam. Carbonated water is also possible. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from Hogwarts is Here and Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Water-repelling Charm

Incantation: Impervius. Light: None. 

Effect: Enchants an object to repel the effects of rain, hail, and other weather conditions. 

Source(s): Canon, with a name from Hogwarts is Here.

Weight-induction Charm

Incantation: Gravato.

Effect: Increases the weight of the target object and its contents (if any). 

Source(s): Hogwarts is Here.

Weight-reduction Charm

Incantation: Relevo.  

Effect: Reduces the weight of the target object and its contents (if any). 

Source(s): Hogwarts is Here.

Weather Normalization Charm

A.K.A. Weather Magic Counterspell

Incantation: Meteolojinx Recanto. 

Effect: Dispels weather-related magic.

Other information: More precise than the General Counterspell, and in particular more effective at handling the chaotic nature of weather-related magic. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from Hogwarts is Here and Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Whirlpool Charm

A.K.A. Water Vortex Charm

Incantation: Vorago.

Effect: Creates one or more whirlpools of various sizes at the target location. 

Other information: Easier to use on less viscous liquids. 

Source(s): Hogwarts is Here. 

Whistling Wand Charm

Incantation: Edo Sonum.  

Effect: Emits a particular sound (by default, a whistle) from the wand, usually at or below the level of human speech. 

Variability: Besides intentional variation in sound, the pitch and timbre may vary between casters.  

Source(s): Hogwarts is Here.

Wind-blowing Jinx

Incantation: Ventus. Light: Gray.

Effect: Blows a gust of air from the tip of the caster’s wand.

Source(s): Canon.

Woodsman's Curse

Incantation: Jehache. Light: Red.

Effect: Delivers multiple successive strikes, like the blow of an axe, against whatever the light hits.

Other effects: The origin of its name is unclear. Some say that it was first used by woodcutters, while others say that it was used by the Woodsman of "Little Red Riding Hood" to kill the story's werewolf antagonist.

Source(s): Original.

Wound-knitting Spell

Incantation: Vulnera Sanentur. Taught in: Healer training. 

Effect: Knits flesh together. 

Other information: Capable of healing deep gashes, halting blood loss, and even treating many wounds caused by Dark magic (emphasis on "treat": it does not heal so much as keep a person alive long enough for them to heal natural way, and scarring is likely). 

Source(s): Witchcraft & Wizardry.
