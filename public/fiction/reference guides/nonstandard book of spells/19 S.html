Severing Charm

Incantation: Diffindo. Light: Light green, pink, or red. 

Effect: Inflicts a precise, accurate cut upon the target.

Variability: Precision can influence the location and depth of the cut. Works best on soft materials like fabric or leaves, but potentially flesh as well, and proficient casting can sever wood or bone, or even stone and metal. 

Other information: Earlier cutting spells had a tendency to burn or shred the target material. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from Hogwarts is Here, Witchcraft & Wizardry, and myself.

Shield Charm

Incantation: Protego. Light: Invisible.

Effect: Deflects spells and blocks physical forces. 

Variability: Possibly, the strength and utility of the charm depends on one's understanding of magic. It is certainly possible to produce inferior shields, as when Harry's Shield Charms were broken by Hermione's "well-placed Jelly-Legs Jinx" (GOF). 

Other information: Not impenetrable. Shield Charms are tangible to some extent: there are several cases of a person being knocked down by a Shield Charm and (in the film) Umbridge was able to block arrows with the Shield Charm. Fairly complex, since many British Ministry employees can't manage it, but Harry and a bunch of other students could manage it, so this certainly isn't insurmountable. Its pendants are post-fixed; unique pendants include Mente Malitiae, which guards against jinxes and most hexes but is permeable to other spells, and Visus, which makes the user harder to concentrate upon but requires complicated wandwork

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration. 

Shield-Breaking Charm

Incantation: Frascia. 

Effect: Ruptures protective magic, like the Shield Charm.

Other information: Limited utility beyond breaking protective charms, but the charm's specialized nature means that it can destroy or pierce many charms that the caster might otherwise find unassailable. Because it is intended to break through protective charms, it is sometimes categorized as a jinx or hex.

Source(s): Original.

Silencing Charm

Incantation: Silencio. Light: None.

Effect: Renders the target mute.

Variability: With precision, the spell may make the target totally incapable of making sound by other means.

Other information: More difficult to perform on ravens than on frogs. It is not always wise to use the Silencing Charm in a duel, because, if your opponent is capable of nonverbal magic, they will no longer broadcast their intent, and this may make up for the difficulty of casting nonverbally.

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration. Additional observations from Blood Crest, by Cauchy (ch. link).

Silencing Charm (Russian)

Incantation: Kaput Molchanus, from Russian капу́т (kapút), "ruin, destruction, end," and молча́ть (molčátʹ), "to keep silent." 

Effect: Renders the target mute.

Other information: Though it is described, and taught, as a Silencing Charm, this spell works by numbing and even paralyzing the vocal cords and other body parts essential to speech. Just as a skilled wix can use the classical Silencing Charm to prevent other kinds of sounds than speech, the skilled practitioner can numb or paralyze other parts of the throat and mouth. Because it is possible to suffocate the target by means of this spell, it is sometimes considered a hex.

Source(s): Tanya Grotter, with significant elaboration.

Spark-spitting Charm

A.K.A. Signaling Spell.

Incantation: Periculum. Light: White.

Effect: Conjures a jet of sparks from the end of the caster's wand, which can reach a great height and hover for a time in the air.

Variability: It is very simple to change the color of the sparks.

Other information: In large quantities, the sparks can make breathing and seeing difficult. Commonly used to signal that the caster is in danger and/or in need of assistance. The sparks set things alight while ascending, but are safe while falling. 

Source(s): Canon, with elaboration from Hogwarts is Here and Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Somnambulist Charm

A.K.A. Puppet-body Charm. 

Incantation: Mobilicorpus. Wand movement: Flick. Light: None.

Effect: Suspends the target body in the air and moves it (somewhat jerkily) as though controlled by puppet strings. 

Other information: If the target is conscious, they will feel a sensation somewhat like strings pulling at their limbs. Even mild physical resistance can break this enchantment, so it is not useful for incapacitation. Often employed by Healers in the course of their day-to-day work. 

Source(s): Canon, with elaboration from Hogwarts is Here and Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Summoning Charm

Incantation: Accio. Light: None.

Effect: Summons the target object, which may be specified by name.

Miscasting: The object will generally navigate around corners but imprecise casting can negatively affect this. The object may be damaged if two people summon it at the same time. 

Other information: Requires thought, and the object must be clear in the caster's mind. Objects which are further away are harder to summon, but in theory there is no hard maximum range. Cannot be used on buildings or living things (except indirectly). Curiously, flobberworms are an exception, and it can also be used on objects being worn or held. There are counter-spells to prevent an object from being Summoned. Most magical goods sold in contemporary times are pre-enchanted with Anti-Theft Charms to prevent them from being Summoned by anyone but their rightful owners.

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from Hogwarts is Here.

Sunbeam Spell

Incantation: Solemos.

Effect: The caster's wand emits a focused beam of pure sunlight, warm to the touch.

Variability: Precision will affect the degree to which the light is "true" sunlight (it is possible, for example, for the light to be sufficient to bother a Devil's Snare but not a vampire). 

Source(s): Canon, with elaboration and a new incantation to distinguish it from the Wand-lighting Charm.

Sweeping Charm

Incantation: Purgo Tabulato.

Other information: Unless provided dustpans, bins, or an aperture through which to sweep, brooms will create piles of dust and debris. Mops may require a source of water. The spell is trickier than it may first appear. 

Source(s): Witchcraft & Wizardry.
