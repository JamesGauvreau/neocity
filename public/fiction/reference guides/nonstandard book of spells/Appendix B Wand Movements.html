Verbal instruction and moving pictures may be the best methods of describing the wand movements necessary to cast a spell, but this only works if somebody is there to guide you in person or the author is a competent artist and has time to draw. Because that isn't always the case, there is a system for "diagramming" the necessary movements. These are imagined as being performed on a two-dimensional square called "the field," as if one were drawing the movements. British diagramming is derived from the French system, and much of the terminology remains French.  

The field is divided into a twenty-five squares called secteurs.

When diagramming the wand strokes of a spell, one begins by describing the shape of the movement, then moves on to the movement's trajectory and then it's end point (if this is in doubt). For example, diagonal: chief-sinister would describe a diagonal stroke to the top-left secteur of the field. If no origin is specified, then the first stroke should begin from mileiu, the centermost secteur. For example, the Bubbling Charm would be diagrammed as "fess, three-quarters circle: sens anti-horaire." 

There are two basic directions: fess, or a horizontal stroke; and pale, or a vertical stroke. Wand strokes move up and to the right by default, so fess sinister indicates a leftward horizontal stroke, and pale base indicates a downward vertical strike. 

Other spatial directions:

  • Circle means "circle," obviously, and demi-circle and quarter-circle mean "half-circle" and "quarter-circle." The standard circle encompasses one-and-a-half secteurs. Its size is decreased or increased by half a secteur with the terms minus (short for minuscule) and majus (short for majuscule), or by a whole secteur with minus double or majus double.   • Sens horaire and sens anti-horaire mean "clockwise" and "anti-clockwise," respectively.

Other directions: 

  • Rapidement means to make the movement very quickly, and lentement means to make it slowly. 
  • Reprise means to repeat the previous stroke. One may also say reprise twice or reprise thrice. 
  • [point: "botte"]

Shorthand: 

  • Bend is shorthand for "from chief-sinister, diagonal: fess," and bend sinister is shorthand for "from chief-dexter, diagonal: fess." If we imagine the bend as extending across two secteurs, then demi-bend extends from the middle of one secteur to its corner, long-bend extends to the corner of the second secteur, and bend double extends to the middle of a third secteur. 

Compendium of Wand Movements

A

Alphabetising Charm: circle sens horaire reprise twice (rapidement). 

B

Banishing Charm: long fess. 

E

Engorgement Charm: pale base (rapidement), circle sens horaire. 

F

Floating Charm: Botte, pale (rapidement).

Full Body-Release Charm: honor dexter (rapidement), circle sens horaire. 

G

Gardening Charm: pale & pale base (rapidement), reprise twice (rapidement).

L

Locking Charm: long fess, demi-pale base, long fess sinister, pale base. 

S

Summoning Charm: demi-circle sens anti-horaire. 

W

Whistling Wand Charm: circle sens horaire.
