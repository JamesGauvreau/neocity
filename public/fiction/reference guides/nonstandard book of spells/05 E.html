Emancipation Charm

Incantation: Emancipare. Taught in: 7th Year Charms.

Effect: Unbars, unbinds, unlocks, unties, or otherwise removes obstacles to freedom.

Other information: The charm is broad in application but difficult to pull off, and will only work if the target object is presently  confining or impeding someone. It requires greater precision against spells. 

Source(s): Witchcraft & Wizardry.

Enchantment-concealing Charm

Incantation: Condidicarmen.

Effect: Hinders many spells and other attempts to detect the presence and nature of an enchantment. 

Variability: The breadth and strength of this spell depends very much upon the caster's knowledge: detection methods of which the caster is unaware are unlikely to be affected much or at all, and those which the caster understands in depth will be affected more severely. 

Source(s): Hogwarts is Here, with elaboration.

Encouragement Charm

Incantation: Hortaminis. 

Effect: Inspires courage in the target. 

Variability: There are many flavors of courage: bravura, daring, fearlessness, spunk, and so forth. 

Miscasting: The target is made more reckless than courageous.  

Other information: The caster must feel courage, to some degree, in order to inspire it in others. It is easier to inspire the same kind of courage felt by oneself, but not necessary.  

Source(s): Hogwarts is Here.

Engorgement Charm

A.K.A. Growing Charm

Incantation: Engorgio. Light: Icy blue, as a circle which expands as it emanates. Taught in: 2nd Year Charms (1985-86). 

Effect: Causes the target, or less commonly a part of the target, to swell immensely.

Other information: If the target is made to swell too much, then it will explode. Can be a counter-charm for the Shrinking Charm. Can be enchanted into food. 

Source(s): Canon, with elaboration from Hogwarts is Here.

Entrail-Evacuating Curse

A.K.A. Entrail-Expelling Curse

Incantation: Unimpedimento. 

Effect: Expels blockages from the intestinal region via forced defecation (e.g. magical enema).

Other information: Rather uncomfortable, and sometimes painful, but can be safer than simply Vanishing the blockage. Invented by Urquhart Rackharrow, a Healer, in the 1600s. Also called the Entrail-Expelling Curse.    

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration.

Entrail-Extracting Curse

A.K.A. Entrail-Expelling Curse

Incantation: Fio extrailis, Fio meaning "become," as in "your entrails will become your extrails." 

Effect: Causes the target's intestines to exit their body—through the rectum, but preexisting abdominal wounds may be used as well

Variability: If performed with care, the Entrail-Extracting Curse will not cause irreparable damage, but a sloppy casting can inflict grievous trauma (including, but not limited to, tearing apart the stomach) and even kill the target. Needless to say, duelists prefer the sloppy version. It is also possible to merely cause abdominal pain or internal damage by causing the intestines to shift in place. There is a variant of this spell which weakens the abdominal tissue so that the intestines burst out as if tearing through gelatin.

Other information: Also called the Entrail-Expelling Curse. To make matters worse, this spell was also invented by Urquhart Rackharrow. Officially, this was meant to be a Healing Spell as well, and there actually are some (very rare) reasons to magically induce rectal prolapse, but Rackharrow was also known for his gambling debts, and it's safe to say that the Entrail-Extracting Curse has seen more use on the streets than at St. Mungo's. Can be enchanted into a waistcoast.   

Source(s): Canon, maybe, with significant elaboration. We know that there's an Entrail-Expelling Curse, but not what it does, so I split the difference: There's one that expels things from your entrails and another that actually expels your entrails.

Explosion Curse

Incantation: Expulso, meaning "to expel." Light: Blue.

Effect: Creates an explosion centered on a point in empty space.

Other information: The force of the explosion is generally sufficient to knock people down but usually doesn't cause significant tissue damage. It does not detonate objects. Modifying the light of this spell is rather simple. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration (the spell’s light is different depending on your current character in the video games, so...why not?), including from Witchcraft & Wizardry, and an incantation from the same source.

Extension Charm

Incantation: Capacious Extremis. 

Effect: Expands the internal dimensions of an object without affecting its external dimensions.

Variability: If the caster is sufficiently skilled, a hundred people could be fit into a space the size of a toilet cubicle.

Other information: Objects in an Extended container are also made lighter, at least usually (it's not inconceivable that an improperly-cast Extension Charm might not provide this benefit). In Britain, Extension Charms are not permitted for the creation of permanent living spaces, but only to contain objects or to serve as temporary living spaces (e.g. tents). Bringing an Extended space into another Extended space is dangerous. 

Source(s): Canon, with some elaboration from Blood Crest, by Cauchy (ch. link), and some speculation.
