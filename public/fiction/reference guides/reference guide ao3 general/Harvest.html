Formatting

Fanfiction.net is a dying nightmarescape held together by duct tape and Faustian bargains, whose founder disappeared years ago when the Devil came to collect his due for reasons that are a mystery to us all. I will speak of it no further except to say that, if that site is dying, then Ficwad is undead. Furthermore, I know nothing of Wattpad or whatever else the kids are posting on today.

All of this is to say that my formatting advice is exclusively for posting on Archive of Our Own. If there are other guides for other websites, then I may add them to this list if you let me know of them.

The Fanwork Research & Reference Guides tag is a useful tag in general, which I recommend that you check if you're writing a crossover.

“Sliding Doors” is a “story” published by AO3 volunteers to demonstrate some of what you can do with normal CSS options available on the site. You can check out their tutorial for more, or look at some of the guides below. If you think that something is missing from one of these lists, then let me know!

  • “Decorations for Fic,” by Jnsn, covers dividers, a chessboard whose pieces move when the board is hovered over, music, chapter titles, image outlines, drop caps, margin images, timetables, and news articles.
  • “The Fic Writer's Guide to Formatting (An AO3 Repository),” by AnisaAnisa, covers HTML, links and images, page dividers, tables, borders, boxes, and customizations of fonts, links, titles, and usernames.
  • “How to Override the Archive's Automated Chapter Headers,” by C Ryan Smith.
  • “How to Use the newly available details and summary code,” by watcherless, shows you how to make clickable spoilers.
  • “The Nice and Accurate Guide to Footnotes,” by Vigs, explains how to make simple footnotes, clickable footnotes that move you around the page, and mouseover footnotes that can be hovered over (or clicked, on mobile).
  • “Ruby Text,” by Elsepth, lets you stack words in superscript above other words.
  • “Skin for Putting Music Indicators in Your Fic,” by SpookyTesting.
  • “Highlight comments of your favourite readers!,” by ElectricAlice.

Posting

If it's been more than a couple of weeks since your last update, then consider including a short recap in the chapter summary or opening notes. If it's been more than a month since your last update, then definitely include a short recap.

If you think that it would be a good idea to make a content warning but you don't want to spoil things for people who don't want to be told about anything, then you can give the content warning in the chapter's end notes, and mention the presence of the content warning in the chapter's beginning notes.

  • "Dates Matter: or why do I care when your work was first published," by snowgall, is about the importance of accurate publishing dates: they're useful not just for fannish historians like myself but for readers, whose expectations will often be shaped by the publication date (e.g. pre-2007 stories have no chance of being DH-compliant).
