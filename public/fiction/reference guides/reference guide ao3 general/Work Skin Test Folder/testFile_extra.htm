Further Nightmares

Callmesalticidae

Summary:

In another universe, The Nightmare Before Christmas was followed by a series of increasingly niche direct-to-video sequels about other holidays. This is a collection of capsule summaries of those videos, arranged in no particular order.

Chapter 1: The Nightmare Before Easter

Chapter Text

Jack Skellington, having regained his zest for Halloween but feeling no less curious about other holidays, decides to visit Easter Town, whose resident rabbits — all of them the children, grandchildren, and other relatives of the Easter Bunny — are just as eager to learn about their visitor. The first act is largely a comedy of errors and cultural misunderstandings, all received in good spirit: for example, when Jack Skellington learns of the Easter association with resurrection, he calls for Doctor Finkelstein and a number of body-snatchers (“resurrectionists”) to demonstrate Halloween Town’s own talent at raising the dead. 

Adversity strikes when the Easter Bunny falls dreadfully ill at a banquet and dies just a few days before she is due to make her annual delivery of Easter Eggs. Jack does his best to settle animosities in the succession crisis that ensues among her many descendants, but disaster is only averted on the third day, when the Easter Bunny hatches from one of her own eggs (the “rabbits” of Easter Town are monotremes). She informs Jack that this happens every year, actually, but appreciates his efforts nonetheless. 

When Jack returns to Halloween Town several rabbits go with him, excited by the opportunity to date somebody that isn’t their cousin.
